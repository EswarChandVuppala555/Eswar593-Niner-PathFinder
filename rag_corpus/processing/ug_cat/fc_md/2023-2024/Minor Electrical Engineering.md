| * * * |

The Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering offers a Minor in Electrical Engineering (ECGR) for non-ECGR majors.

All program-level Admissions and Progression Requirements are in addition to the [University of North Carolina at Charlotte Admission Requirements](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/content.php?catoid=36&navoid=4045). |
| ## Admission Requirements<br>* * *<br>#### Current UNC Charlotte Undergraduate Students<br>- See [University Admission Requirements](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/content.php?catoid=36&navoid=4045)<br>## Prerequisite Courses<br>* * *<br>The Minor in Electrical Engineering assumes that students have a background in mathematics and physics equivalent to that covered in the first two years of the ECGR curriculum, which includes the following courses:<br>- [MATH 1241 - Calculus I](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (3)<br>- [MATH 1242 - Calculus II](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (3)<br>- [MATH 2171 - Differential Equations](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (3)<br>- [PHYS 2101 - Physics for Science and Engineering I](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (3)<br>- [PHYS 2101L - Physics for Science and Engineering I Laboratory](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (1)<br>- [PHYS 2102 - Physics for Science and Engineering II](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (3)<br>## Minor Requirements<br>* * *<br>A Minor in Electrical Engineering consists of 18 credit hours and requires the following:<br>### Core Courses (9 credit hours)<br>* * *<br>- [ECGR 2111 - Network Theory I](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (3)<br>-     or [ECGR 2161 - Basic Electrical Engineering I](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (3)<br>- [ECGR 2112 - Network Theory II](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (3)<br>- [ECGR 2254 - Analytical Foundations of Electrical and Computer Engineering](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (3)<br>### Advanced Courses (6 credit hours)<br>* * *<br>_Select at least two of the following._ _If all three listed courses are taken, one of the courses may count toward the elective course requirement below._<br>- [ECGR 3111 - Signals and Systems](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (3)<br>- [ECGR 3121 - Introduction to Electromagnetic Fields](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (3)<br>- [ECGR 3131 - Fundamentals of Electronics and Semiconductors](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_program.php?catoid=36&poid=9925#) (3)<br>### Elective Course (3 credit hours)<br>* * *<br>_If only two advanced courses were selected above, select an elective course from the following.  All prerequisites for the selected elective courses must be satisfied._<br>- [ECGR 3XXX - Electrical and Computer Engineering Elective (3)](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_content.php?catoid=36&nav_oid=4094)<br>- [ECGR 4XXX - Electrical and Computer Engineering Elective (3)](https://catalog.charlotte.edu/preview_content.php?catoid=36&nav_oid=4095)<br>## Total = 18 Credit Hours<br>* * *<br>## Progression Requirements<br>* * *<br>Students must achieve a minimum GPA of 2.0 in all Minor courses.<br>## Special Policies or Requirements<br>* * *<br>This minor is not available for a student who has a major in either Electrical Engineering or Computer Engineering. |
